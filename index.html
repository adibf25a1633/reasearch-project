Case Study

Elaeis guineensis FFB Growth Dynamics

In Malaysia, optimizing the harvest of Oil Palm Fresh Fruit Bunches (FFB) is crucial. Harvesting too early results in low Oil Extraction Rates (OER), while harvesting too late leads to high Free Fatty Acid (FFA) decay. Agrotech students use calculus to find the absolute point of maximum growth velocity and maturity plateau.

Biomathematical Model

Logistic Curve
Cumulative Growth Function f(t)
f(t) = L ÷ (1 + e-k(t - t₀))

Predicts cumulative wet weight (kg) of the FFB at week t post-anthesis (pollination).

Instantaneous Growth Rate f'(t)
f'(t) = (L · k · e-k(t - t₀)) ÷ (1 + e-k(t - t₀))2

The first derivative of weight with respect to time. This measures the instantaneous growth velocity (kg/week).
